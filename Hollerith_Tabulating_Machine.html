Hollerith Tabulating Machine

Machines

Dates: 1884

Why Significant

Throughout the 1880s, the U.S. population was rapidly expanding. This caused a problem, as the government realized that the time it would take to count the 1890 census could exceed 10 years, which would be when the next census would be taken. The Hollerith Tabulating Machine was able to solve this problem, as it could tabulate the population data much faster.

History

The Hollerith Tabulating Machine was invented by Herman Hollerith in 1884. The machine used punched cards to do tabulations on large datasets. This was invented, because U.S. census data needed to be calculated faster to account for the nation's expanding population. The machine was able to cut the time it took to calculate the census data by two years. Hollerith's company, through mergers and aquisitions, eventually became IBM.
