Biography

Mary Kenneth Keller was a pioneering computer scientist and educator who made significant contributions to the field of CS. She was born in 1913 in Cleveland, Ohio and grew up in a supportive and intellectually stimulating enviornment. Keller attended DeSales High School in Toledo, Ohio where she developed a passion for math and science. She pursued a higher education at DeSales College, where she earned a Bachelors degree in math. Keller then continued her studies at the University of wisconsin, where she earned her Phd. After completeing her education Keller began a career in academia, She taught at various universities. She was known was known for her innovative teaching methods and her ability to inspire and motivate her students. She was instrumental in the creation of BASIC programming languages. Her contributions to the field of CS and her dedication to education contiue to be celebrated and inspier future generations of women in technology.
