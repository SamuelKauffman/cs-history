IBM Model 5150

Dates: Developed in 1981

Made use of off-the-shelf parts

The 5150 was developed by IBM under Don Eskridge. It was created using off-the-shelf parts which allowed it to be cheaply manufactured. It essentially opened the personal computing market in America.

History

The IBM Model 5150 finished development in 1981 under Don Eskridge at IBM. It was released August 12th of that year. It was primarily intended for serious business tasks but also had the capability to play games and other programs.

The Model 5150 started at $1565 ($5040 in 2022 adjusted for inflation) and included:

  • 40 kB ROM
  • Up to two 5.25 inch floppy drives
  • Monochromatic display
  • Optional cassette drive

The Model 5150 revolutionized computing by becoming the first widely adopted personal computer. It put personal computing into the mainstream and was followed by a subsequent boom in adoption.
